Product

TeleHealthRobotics is creating the world’s first tele-robotic healthcare platform that promises to bring down the cost of healthcare delivery by capitalizing on efficiency gains from technology. You may know of ultrasound from its prevalence in prenatal care. Beyond the uterus, it is used to image the heart, vascular system, brain, and abdomen. The ways in which it is used are expanding rapidly from prevention to detection to procedure image guidance, procedure follow-up, and chronic care. Once a tool relegated primarily to the hospital, the number of locations in which ultrasound can now be used is expanding greatly, including everywhere from the clinic to the battlefield to the procedure room to the pharmacy.

TeleHealthRobotics uses TRUDI: Tele-Robotic Ultrasound for Distance Imaging. TRUDI leverages the convergence of low cost robotics and control systems with advanced tele-presence. It allows an experienced operator located anywhere in the world to operate a diagnostic ultrasound system at a distance.

News and Studies

Trans-Atlantic Ultrasound

Dr. Partho Sengupta, a cardiologist at Mount Sinai Medical Center, recently performed a real-time ultrasound exam in Boston. But Dr. Sengupta wasn’t in Boston at the time — he was in Germany, collaborating with the German Heart Center in Munich. Dr. Sengupta used a generic desktop in Germany and TeleHealthRobotics’ hardware in Boston to carry out the ultrasound. The results of this study were published in the prestigious Journal of the College of American Cardiology: Imaging.

Study:  Feasibility of Intercity and Trans-Atlantic Telerobotic Remote Ultrasound: Assessment Facilitated by a Nondedicated Bandwidth Connection.
